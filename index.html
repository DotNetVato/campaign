About James Beller

James Beller has lived in Kenosha County his entire life. Growing up in Paddock Lake, he attended local schools before enlisting in the United States Army. After his Army service, he returned to Paddock while he attended college. He built his career here, raised his family here, and has spent nearly three decades serving and protecting the people of Kenosha County. His commitment to public safety is not a slogan. It is measured in years of leadership, responsibility, and results.

A United States Army veteran, Beller began his public safety career serving Kenosha County as a dispatcher with the Kenosha Joint Services Communications Center before joining the Kenosha County Sheriff's Office, where he worked as an undercover narcotics agent. In January 2001, he joined the City of Kenosha Police Department, beginning what would become a nearly three-decade career in law enforcement leadership.

Throughout his career, Beller has focused on strengthening public safety institutions from the inside out. He has modernized operations, improved accountability, and built specialized units to ensure serious crimes are investigated professionally and thoroughly. His leadership philosophy is simple: strong organizations, clear standards, and disciplined execution are what keep communities safe.

As Sheriff, Beller will bring the same professional, steady leadership to the Kenosha County Sheriff's Office, ensuring the agency remains trusted, effective, and prepared for the future.
