My Promise to Kenosha County

Meet James Beller

James Beller is a lifelong Kenosha County resident with nearly three decades of service protecting this community. His commitment to the Kenosha County Sheriff's Office began early as a member of the Sheriff's Office Youth Explorer program and continued through direct service as an undercover narcotics agent. Public safety is not new to him, and it is not political. It has been his life's work.

A United States Army veteran, Beller served Kenosha County as a dispatcher with the Kenosha Joint Services Communications Center before joining the City of Kenosha Police Department in 2001. There, he built a career in investigations and command leadership, overseeing complex cases, specialized units, and major public safety initiatives that demanded accountability, discipline, and results.

Throughout his career, Beller has focused on strengthening public safety from the inside out. He has modernized operations, reinforced professional standards, and built specialized teams to ensure serious crimes are investigated thoroughly and responsibly. His leadership philosophy is straightforward: clear expectations, personal accountability, and disciplined execution are what keep communities safe.

As Sheriff, James Beller will bring steady, experienced leadership to the Kenosha County Sheriff's Office. He will support deputies and staff, enforce the law fairly, and ensure the agency remains trusted, effective, and prepared to meet the challenges ahead.

Why He Is Running

James Beller is running for Kenosha County Sheriff because the office demands experienced, disciplined leadership and a clear vision for the future. The Sheriff's Office is responsible for jail operations, court security, patrol, investigations, and countywide public safety. That responsibility requires thoughtful leadership prepared to guide the organization forward.

Beller brings both deep local experience and a broad leadership perspective. He worked undercover for the Kenosha County Sheriff's Office from October 1998 through January 2001 and served on the Sheriff's Tactical Response Team from 2007 through 2010. Over nearly three decades in law enforcement, he has risen from patrol officer to Police Captain, leading investigative divisions, training programs, and regional critical-incident teams. He has managed people, budgets, policy, and high-risk operations, and worked closely with local, state, and federal partners to protect the community.

Kenosha County deserves a Sheriff who understands the organization, respects its people, and is prepared to lead it into the future. James Beller is running to provide steady leadership, clear standards, and professional accountability across every function of the Sheriff's Office.

Regional and Inter-Agency Leadership

Wisconsin law requires that officer-involved shootings and in-custody deaths be investigated by an independent outside agency.
